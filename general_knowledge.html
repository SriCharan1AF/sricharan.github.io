General Knowledge (Code No. 02) for CDS (Combined Defence Services)

The General Knowledge (Code No. 02) syllabus for the CDS (Combined Defence Services) exam is designed to assess a candidate's awareness of current events, general awareness, and their understanding of topics related to history, geography, science, and other subjects. This section does not require specialized study but focuses on what an educated person is expected to know from observation and everyday learning.

Here is a detailed breakdown of the General Knowledge syllabus for the CDS exam:

1. Current Events

This section tests a candidate’s knowledge of significant events happening around the world. The following topics are typically covered:

  • National and International Affairs: Important events within the country and internationally, including political developments, treaties, agreements, summits, and notable actions by global organizations like the UN, WHO, etc.
  • Sports: Recent sports events, achievements, winners of major national and international tournaments, Olympics, Asian Games, Commonwealth Games, etc.
  • Awards and Honors: Major awards in literature, films, sports, science, and arts. Examples include the Nobel Prize, Bharat Ratna, Padma Awards, Booker Prize, and Academy Awards.
  • Government Schemes: Key government initiatives and schemes such as Make in India, Digital India, Ayushman Bharat, Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, etc.
  • Economy: Updates on the Indian economy, budgets, new economic policies, inflation, growth rates, and important financial institutions like the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), World Bank, International Monetary Fund (IMF), etc.
  • Science and Technology: Recent advancements and discoveries in science and technology, space exploration, new innovations, ISRO missions, and technological developments.

2. Indian History

This section covers the history of India from ancient to modern times. It includes:

Ancient History:
  • Indus Valley Civilization: Key aspects of the Harappan culture, towns, trade, religion, and social structure.
  • Vedic Period: Society, economy, and religious practices during the early and later Vedic periods.
  • Maurya and Gupta Empires: Achievements of rulers like Ashoka and Chandragupta Maurya, governance, administration, the spread of Buddhism, and the golden age of the Gupta period.

Medieval History:
  • Delhi Sultanate: Key dynasties like the Slave Dynasty, Khaljis, Tughlaqs, and Lodis. Administrative systems, architecture, and cultural influence.
  • Mughal Empire: Significant rulers like Akbar, Aurangzeb, Mughal administration, and the cultural developments under Mughal rule.

Modern History:
  • Colonial India: The arrival of Europeans, East India Company, major battles (Plassey, Buxar), and British rule.
  • Indian Freedom Struggle: Events, movements, and key figures involved in India’s independence struggle, including:
    • Revolt of 1857.
    • Indian National Congress and its role.
    • Swadeshi Movement, Non-Cooperation Movement, Civil Disobedience Movement, and Quit India Movement.
    • Contributions of Mahatma Gandhi, Subhash Chandra Bose, Bhagat Singh, and other freedom fighters.
  • Post-Independence India: Important events and policies after India gained independence in 1947, including constitutional developments.

3. Geography

The geography section assesses a candidate’s understanding of the physical and political aspects of India and the world.

Physical Geography:
  • Earth and Solar System: Basic knowledge about the solar system, the shape of the Earth, latitudes, longitudes, and the concept of time zones.
  • Climate: Types of climates, factors influencing the climate, monsoons, global climatic zones, and weather patterns.
  • Landforms: Major landforms like mountains, plateaus, plains, rivers, and deserts, both in India and globally.
  • Oceans and Seas: Major oceans, seas, currents, and their significance in global geography.
  • Soil and Vegetation: Different types of soils found in India, types of vegetation, forests, and agricultural practices.

Indian Geography:
  • Physical Features: India's mountain ranges (Himalayas, Western and Eastern Ghats), major rivers (Ganga, Yamuna, Brahmaputra, Godavari, etc.), deserts (Thar Desert), and coastal plains.
  • Resources: Natural resources of India, including mineral resources (iron, coal, petroleum), forest resources, and agricultural crops.
  • Economic Geography: Major industries, agriculture, trade, transportation networks (railways, roads, ports), and their significance to the Indian economy.

World Geography:
  • Continents and Countries: Geographical features of various continents, major countries, important cities, and significant geographical landmarks.

4. Indian Polity

This section focuses on the political system and governance structure of India. It includes:

  • Constitution of India: Basic structure of the Constitution, key articles, and amendments.
  • Fundamental Rights and Duties: Rights and duties enshrined in the Constitution, Directive Principles of State Policy.
  • Parliament of India: Composition, powers, and functions of the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and Rajya Sabha (Council of States).
  • Executive Branch: Structure and powers of the President, Prime Minister, and the Council of Ministers.
  • Judiciary: Powers and structure of the Indian judicial system, including the Supreme Court, High Courts, and subordinate courts.
  • Federalism in India: Relations between the central and state governments, division of powers, and important schedules.
  • Election Process: Election Commission of India, electoral process, representation, and recent developments in elections.

5. Economics

This section evaluates the candidate’s understanding of basic economic concepts and the Indian economy.

  • Basic Economic Concepts: GDP, GNP, inflation, deflation, economic growth, and development.
  • Indian Economic Planning: Five-year plans, NITI Aayog, public sector undertakings, and privatization.
  • Banking and Financial Systems: RBI, commercial banks, monetary policy, fiscal policy, budget, and taxation.
  • Current Economic Issues: Recent economic developments, reforms, and government initiatives such as GST, demonetization, and financial inclusion programs.

6. Science and Technology

This section tests the candidate’s knowledge of basic scientific concepts, recent innovations, and discoveries in science.

Physics:
  • Laws of motion, work, energy, and power.
  • Concepts of electricity and magnetism.
  • Optics, sound waves, and heat transfer.

Chemistry:
  • Basic concepts like atoms, molecules, and chemical reactions.
  • Properties of gases, liquids, and solids.
  • Important chemical compounds and everyday applications.

Biology:
  • Basic knowledge of human anatomy and physiology.
  • Important concepts related to plant and animal kingdoms.
  • Diseases, their causes, and prevention.

Recent Scientific Developments:
  • Space exploration, satellite missions (ISRO, NASA).
  • Biotechnology, genetic engineering, and environmental science.

7. Defence-Related Knowledge

Since the CDS exam is for candidates aspiring to join the defense forces, questions related to defense may also be asked. Topics include:

  • Defense Forces in India:
    • Structure, ranks, and key roles of the Army, Navy, and Air Force.
  • Important Military Exercises:
    • National and international joint exercises.
  • Defense Technology:
    • Recent developments in defense equipment, missiles, and weapons systems (e.g., BrahMos, INS Vikrant).

8. Miscellaneous Topics

Other areas where candidates are tested include:

  • Environmental Science:
    • Ecology, biodiversity, pollution, and sustainable development.
  • Art and Culture:
    • Indian dance forms, music, painting, architecture, and important cultural institutions.

Key Focus Areas for Preparation
  • Stay Updated on Current Affairs:
    • Regular reading of newspapers, magazines, and following news channels helps in keeping up with current events and general awareness.
  • Refer to Standard Textbooks:
    • Books like NCERTs for History, Geography, Science, and Polity provide a good foundation for static portions of the syllabus.
  • Revise Previous Year Papers:
    • Solving past CDS General Knowledge papers gives an idea of the type of questions asked and helps with time management during the exam.
