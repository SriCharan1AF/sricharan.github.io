English Syllabus for CDS (Combined Defence Services)

The English syllabus for CDS (Combined Defence Services) focuses on assessing a candidate’s grasp of the language, their understanding of various topics, and their ability to use the language effectively. The question paper is typically designed to test candidates' understanding of English grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and their overall language usage.

Here is a detailed breakdown of the English syllabus for the CDS exam:

1. Vocabulary

The vocabulary section tests the candidates' understanding and command over English words, synonyms, antonyms, and usage of idiomatic expressions. The following areas are usually covered:

  • Synonyms: Candidates will be given a word and will have to choose a word that is closest in meaning to the given word.
  • Antonyms: Similar to synonyms, candidates will be required to select the word that is opposite in meaning to the given word.
  • Word Meanings: Questions to test the meaning and usage of words. This may include lesser-known words, to test the depth of the candidate's vocabulary.
  • Idioms and Phrases: Common idiomatic expressions and phrases, where candidates are required to understand their meaning and usage.
  • One-word Substitutions: Candidates will be asked to substitute a group of words or a phrase with a single word.

2. Grammar and Usage

This section assesses the candidates' understanding of English grammar rules, including correct sentence formation and syntax. It includes:

  • Parts of Speech: Understanding the function of words (nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, conjunctions, prepositions, interjections) and how they are used in sentences.
  • Tenses: Correct usage of present, past, and future tenses. This includes simple, continuous, perfect, and perfect continuous tenses.
  • Subject-Verb Agreement: Ensuring that the verb in a sentence agrees with the subject in terms of number and person.
  • Articles and Determiners: Correct use of articles (a, an, the) and determiners (some, any, much, many, little, few, etc.).
  • Prepositions: Proper use of prepositions (in, on, at, by, with, under, over, etc.) in sentences.
  • Conjunctions: Usage of coordinating, subordinating, and correlative conjunctions to connect clauses and sentences.
  • Active and Passive Voice: Conversion of sentences from active to passive voice and vice versa.
  • Direct and Indirect Speech (Narration): Conversion of sentences from direct speech to indirect speech and vice versa.
  • Error Spotting: Identifying and correcting grammatical errors in given sentences or passages.

3. Reading Comprehension

This section tests the candidate’s ability to understand, interpret, and analyze written passages. The following areas are covered:

  • Passage-based Questions: A passage of text is provided, and the candidate has to answer questions based on its content. This includes understanding the central idea, tone of the author, inference, factual information, etc.
  • Contextual Word Meaning: Finding the meaning of words or phrases as used in the passage.
  • Logical Inferences: Drawing conclusions or inferences based on the information provided in the passage.
  • Theme and Tone of the Passage: Identifying the main theme or the author’s tone (informative, critical, satirical, etc.) of the passage.

4. Sentence Arrangement

This section tests a candidate’s ability to logically arrange sentences to form a coherent paragraph:

  • Jumbled Sentences (Para Jumbles): Candidates are given a set of sentences in random order, which they need to arrange logically to form a meaningful paragraph.
  • Paragraph Completion: Candidates may be given an incomplete paragraph and asked to choose the most suitable sentence that completes it logically.

5. Sentence Improvement

This section tests the ability to identify and correct incorrect or inappropriate usage in sentences:

  • Sentence Correction: A sentence with grammatical or syntactical errors is provided, and candidates are required to identify the error and choose the correct option to improve the sentence.
  • Phrase Substitution: In this, a part of the sentence may be underlined, and the candidate has to choose an option that replaces the underlined part with a more suitable or grammatically correct alternative.

6. Fill in the Blanks

This section tests the candidate’s ability to choose the correct word or phrase to fill in the blanks within sentences. The areas covered include:

  • Grammar-based Fillers: Fill in the blank with grammatically correct prepositions, articles, conjunctions, etc.
  • Vocabulary-based Fillers: Candidates are expected to select the most contextually appropriate word from the given options to complete the sentence.

7. Spelling Test

In this section, candidates are tested on their knowledge of correct spelling of words. They will be given a word, and they must identify the correct or incorrect spelling.

8. Cloze Test

A Cloze Test involves a passage with several blanks, where candidates are required to fill in the blanks with the most appropriate words. This tests their understanding of the passage's context and the logical flow of ideas.

Key Focus Areas for Preparation

  • Reading Practice: Candidates should practice reading English newspapers, magazines, and novels to improve comprehension and vocabulary. Editorials in newspapers are especially helpful in understanding various themes and tones.
  • Grammar Mastery: Familiarity with basic grammar rules is crucial for solving questions related to sentence improvement, error spotting, and fill in the blanks. Candidates can practice from grammar books or take online quizzes for regular revision.
  • Vocabulary Building: Regular reading, learning new words, and practicing synonyms, antonyms, and idioms are important for scoring well in vocabulary-based questions.
  • Previous Year Papers: Solving previous CDS English question papers gives an idea of the pattern, types of questions, and the difficulty level of the exam.
  • Mock Tests: Regular mock tests will help in time management, increasing speed, and accuracy in solving the paper.

Exam Pattern for CDS English Paper

  • The English paper for CDS is usually of 100 marks.
  • The total duration of the paper is 2 hours.
  • The questions are of objective type (Multiple Choice Questions).
  • Negative marking is applicable for incorrect answers.

By focusing on these areas, candidates can strengthen their command of English and perform well in the CDS exam.
