Mathematics (Code No. 03) for CDS (Combined Defence Services)

The Mathematics syllabus (Code No. 03) for the CDS (Combined Defence Services) exam focuses on testing a candidate’s understanding of basic mathematical concepts. The level of questions is typically aligned with the mathematics taught up to the 10th grade. The questions aim to assess problem-solving abilities, logical reasoning, and numerical aptitude.

Here is a detailed breakdown of the Mathematics syllabus for the CDS exam:

1. Arithmetic

This section focuses on basic arithmetic concepts and problem-solving using these concepts. It includes:

  • Number System:
    • Natural numbers, Integers, Rational numbers, and Real numbers.
    • Properties of numbers: odd, even, prime, composite, etc.
    • Divisibility rules (tests of divisibility by 2, 3, 4, 5, 9, 11).
    • HCF (Highest Common Factor) and LCM (Least Common Multiple).
    • Euclidean Algorithm.
  • Fundamental Operations: Basic operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
  • Square Roots and Cube Roots: Calculation and properties of square roots and cube roots.
  • Decimal Fractions: Conversion between fractions and decimals, performing operations with decimal numbers.
  • Percentage: Calculation of percentage, increase and decrease, applications in profit & loss, discount, etc.
  • Ratio and Proportion: Concepts of ratio, direct and inverse proportion, and their applications.
  • Time, Work, and Distance: Questions related to the calculation of speed, time, distance, and work efficiency.
  • Simple and Compound Interest: Calculating interest, amount, and related concepts over a period of time.

2. Elementary Number Theory

This section is based on fundamental number theory concepts:

  • Division Algorithm: The relationship between divisors, quotients, and remainders.
  • Prime and Composite Numbers: Understanding prime numbers, composite numbers, and methods to find primes.
  • Factorization Theorem: Decomposition of numbers into prime factors.
  • Multiples and Factors: Identifying multiples and factors of a number.
  • Logarithms: Logarithmic functions, laws of logarithms (product, quotient, and power rules), and the use of logarithmic tables.

3. Algebra

This section assesses the understanding of algebraic expressions, equations, and their applications:

  • Basic Operations: Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of algebraic expressions.
  • Simple Factors and Factorization: Factorization of algebraic expressions using methods such as grouping, difference of squares, and the quadratic form.
  • HCF and LCM of Polynomials: Finding the highest common factor and least common multiple of polynomials.
  • Theory of Polynomials: Roots of polynomials and the relation between coefficients and roots of quadratic equations.
  • Remainder Theorem: Understanding the remainder theorem and its application in finding the remainder of polynomial division.
  • Quadratic Equations: Solutions of quadratic equations using factorization, the quadratic formula, and completing the square.
  • Simultaneous Linear Equations: Solving simultaneous linear equations in two variables by substitution, elimination, and graphical methods.
  • Set Language and Notation: Representation of sets, types of sets (finite, infinite, equal, universal), and operations on sets (union, intersection, complement).
  • Rational Expressions and Conditional Identities: Simplification and operations with rational expressions.
  • Laws of Indices: Understanding and applying the laws of indices (exponent rules) for simplifying expressions.

4. Trigonometry

This section focuses on the basics of trigonometry and its applications in solving various problems:

  • Trigonometric Ratios: Understanding sine, cosine, and tangent of an angle.
  • Values of Trigonometric Functions: Memorizing the values of sin, cos, and tan for standard angles (0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90°).
  • Basic Trigonometric Identities: Using fundamental identities such as:
    • sin2 θ + cos2 θ = 1
    • sin θ / cos θ = tan θ
  • Use of Trigonometric Tables: Understanding how to use trigonometric tables for calculating the values of trigonometric functions.
  • Heights and Distances: Solving problems related to heights and distances using trigonometric ratios (angle of elevation and depression).

5. Geometry

This section deals with the properties of different geometrical figures and their relationships:

  • Lines and Angles: Understanding properties of lines, angles at a point, adjacent angles, and vertically opposite angles.
  • Triangles:
    • Properties of triangles (isosceles, equilateral, right-angled triangles).
    • Theorems related to sides and angles, congruence, and similarity of triangles.
    • Theorems on the sum of angles of a triangle, exterior angle theorem.
  • Circles: Properties of a circle, tangents, chords, arcs, and sectors.
  • Parallelograms, Rectangles, Squares: Properties of parallelograms, squares, and rectangles, including relationships between diagonals and angles.
  • Loci: Understanding the concept of loci and solving simple locus problems.

6. Mensuration

This section focuses on the measurement of areas, surface areas, and volumes of different geometrical figures:

  • Areas: Calculation of the area of basic plane figures such as squares, rectangles, triangles, parallelograms, and circles.
  • Surface Areas and Volumes:
    • Surface area and volume of 3D figures like cuboids, cylinders, cones, and spheres.
    • Lateral surface area and total surface area of solids.

7. Statistics

This section deals with the collection, analysis, and interpretation of numerical data:

  • Collection and Tabulation of Data: Methods of collecting data, tabulating, and organizing data in frequency tables.
  • Graphical Representation:
    • Drawing and interpreting bar graphs, histograms, frequency polygons, and pie charts.
